Headline yield is only the starting point. What matters is how much cash actually qualifies for that rate, what hoops are required, and how quickly the bank can change the terms.

## What I check first

1. Bonus conditions. Salary crediting, card spend, and bill payments can make an advertised rate look better than it really is.
2. Balance cap. A strong rate on the first tranche of cash is less useful if the rest of the money earns close to zero.
3. Base rate versus promo rate. Temporary boosts are fine, but I want to know the steady-state return.
4. Liquidity. If the account has lockups, long transfer times, or withdrawal penalties, I treat it differently from a true emergency-fund account.

## My rule of thumb

If an account needs too many conditions to stay competitive, I would rather accept a slightly lower rate for something simple and predictable.
