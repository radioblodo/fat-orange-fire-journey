Earlier this week, on 21 Mar 2026, it was reported on CNN that the price of gold dropped by 11% and the article even call it the worst week for gold since 1983. Suddenly, gold became a hot topic once again. This is an interesting phenomenon because traditionally, most people would think that in times of chaos, like the situation in the Middle East now, safe assets like gold should rise in value since gold is often seen as the safe haven. But why is the reality so different? I have consulted AI and read articles online. The main reasons are outlined below. 

---
## Why did gold price drop? 

## Reason 1: High Interest Rate (Hawkish Outlook) 
While the Fed did not announce a hike in interest rate, most people expect there to be no interest rate cut due to the inflation caused by supply chain disruptions, especially due to the shortage of oil and liquefied natural gas (LNG). The Fed did raise the 2026 inflation forecast to 2.7%. As a result of this, most people expect the interest rate to remain steady at around 3.5% ~ 3.7%, making the opportunity cost to hold gold higher as it would be better to hold US bonds (higher interest payouts) than holding gold that is not generating any income. Anyways, most people have already made some money due to the rise in gold value prior to this, it makes sense to sell out gold now to lock in the gains. 

### Reason 2: Margin Call Liquidity Squeeze 
This is not as significant a reason compared to reason 1 but I feel that it could contribute partially to the drop in gold price. As oil prices rise sharply due to the conflict in Middle East, there are some institutional investors who were previously short on oil positions that are losing money. As such, they need to cover their losses and meet margin calls. It makes sense for these people to sell gold at a record high price to recover cash to meet these margin calls. 

### Reason 3: Strong US Dollar 
I just found out recently from Gemini about this index called the US Dollar Index (DXY), which is a measure of the value of the US dollar to a basket of 6 major foreign currencies (Euro, Japanese Yen, British Pound, Canadian Dollar, Swedish Krona, Swiss Franc). It is a benchmark for the overall strength or weakness of the US dollar in the foreign exchange market. 

Ok anyways, DXY has broken 100 and thus global capital is rushing into the dollar because US dollar is seen as the safe haven, which contributes to the dropping of gold price.

## Should I buy gold now? 

Honestly, I don't have a clear answer to this question and I think there is a thousand answers to this question depending on the personal circumstances. However, I do think that it is important to have some gold in my portfolio as I have too little of that now (< 5%). Below are my key considerations, with no regards for the price of the gold for now since it is impossible to time the market. 

### Reason 1: Gold can be used for bartering in times of crisis 
In World War 2, when fiat currencies were practically useless due to hyperinflation, the main method of getting food from the black market was through bartering and the main exchange of value was done using precious metals like gold and silver. Thus, there is definitely value in these precious metals still, but how much I don't know and I don't think anyone in this world knows since there is no intrinsic value in gold. However, the primary reason why gold and silver were used for bartering during war and in ancient times, is exactly because gold is rare, limited in quantity and cannot be artificially produced. I do see some reports online claiming that it is possible to artificially produce gold, but as far as I understand, that is done at a very high price and not at scale like how diamonds can be produced. Thus, from the perspective of having some gold to protect yourself in times of crisis, it is reasonable to buy some gold at a **reasonable** price. 

### Reason 2: Gold is a good hedge against inflation 
There is a famous saying that says that in Ancient Rome, 1 ounce of gold can buy you fine [toga](https://www.historyhit.com/togas-and-tunics-what-did-ancient-romans-wear/) and today 1 ounce of gold can still allow you to buy a high quality tailored suit. I watched a [video](https://youtu.be/foLPERpzRzI?si=lfk36yWI-PbT_0Ay) that illustrates a similar idea using cow. So from this perspective, even if we do not consider the possibility of making gains from gold, buying gold will likely to at least presever the value of our investments, provided the horizon is long enough. Thus, I think it is still reasonable to hold some gold. Of course, this depends a lot on the entry price and how long you intend to hold gold for.
